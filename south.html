GEMS OF SOUTH INDIA !!!



Region Where The Ocean Rules, Known For Its Vast Culture, Tradition And Monumental Beauties






Mysore

Mysore is one of the major cities in the South Indian state of Karnataka. Noted for its heritage structures,palaces and for the festivities that take place during the Dasara festival.Mysore Palace is an incredible man-made edifice. The city is famous for its silk and is also a thriving sandalwood and incense center.

Must Visit:

  • Mysuru Palace
  • Sri Chamundeshwari Temple
  • Sri Chamarajendra Zoological Gardens
  • Somnathpur Temple
  • Devaraja Market
  • Namdroling Monastery
  • Venugopala Swamy Temple
  • Brindavan Garden
  • Melkote
  • Sri Nandi Temple



    • Coorg

    Coorg, also known as Kodagu, is a beautiful hill station in the Indian state of Karnataka. It is famous for its coffee plantations, steep hills, countless streams, rich flora & fauna, lush forests and breathtaking views. The best way to experience the coffee plantations is to stay at one of the many homestays and resorts that are situated on coffee estates.

    Must Visit:

  • Tadiandamol Peak
  • Iruppu Falls
  • Dubare Elephant Camp
  • Raja's Seat
  • Abbey Falls
  • Mercara Gold Estate Coffee Plantation
  • Cauvery River
  • Tibetian Monastery
  • Brahmagiri Hill
  • Pushpagiri Wildlife Sanctuary



    • Goa

    Goa, also known as the 'Pearl of the Orient', is located on the western coast of India in the coastal belt known as Konkan. Goa is one of the most favorite destination among Indian tourists due to its pristine beaches. From shopping at the various markets, to eating the local cuisine and water sports galore, Goa is well-known for a lot of unique offerings.

    Must Visit:

  • Baga Beach
  • Palolem Beach
  • Mangeshi Temple
  • Fontainhas
  • Calangute Beach
  • Reis Magos Fort
  • St. Alex Church
  • Candolim Beach
  • The Saturday Night Market
  • Church of Our Lady Of Hope



    • Munnar

    Munnar the exotic hill station in Kerala is blessed generously by the nature.It has a pleasant and favourable climate throughout the year. Unending expanse of tea plantations pristine valleys and mountains exotic species of flora and fauna in its wild sanctuaries and forests aroma of spice scented cool air,just name it the place is like a slice of heaven.

    Must Visit:

  • Tea Gardens
  • Top Station
  • Rajamalai (Eravikulam) National Park
  • Kalari Kshethra
  • Anamudi Peak
  • Mattupetty Dam
  • Chinar Wildlife Sanctuary
  • Thoovanam Falls
  • Kundala Dam Lake
  • Attukal Waterfalls



    • Ooty

    Ooty deservedly earns its reputation as the 'Queen of Hill Stations' for its extensive tea plantations,lakes and other natural splendours.Nature has been generous with this region,by far the most beautiful in the state. Connoisseurs will find plenty to choose from: dust and leaf;black,green,black and white varieties;and flavours such as masala,jasmine,cardamom and even chocolate.

    Must Visit:

  • Nilgiri Mountain Railway
  • Pykara Falls
  • Upper Bhavani Lake
  • Doddabetta Peak
  • Avalanche Lake
  • Mudumalai National Park
  • Emerald Lake
  • Tea Museum
  • Ooty Lake
  • Elk Hill Murugan Temple
